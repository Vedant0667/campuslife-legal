CampusLife Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how CampusLife ("CampusLife," "we," "us"), operated by [LEGAL ENTITY NAME], collects, uses, shares, and protects your information in the CampusLife mobile app (the "Service"). We will post any material changes here and update the date above; significant changes will also be surfaced in the app.

Plain-language summary: A college student logs a short daily wellbeing check-in; a parent the student chooses to link with can see those check-ins and trends over time. We treat your wellbeing data as sensitive health information. We do not sell your data, show ads, or use third-party analytics or tracking. You can delete your account and all of your data at any time, from within the app.

1. Who we are & how to contact us

Data controller: [LEGAL ENTITY NAME], [ADDRESS]. Privacy contact: privacy@campuslife.app. [If you have EU/UK users, name an EU/UK representative and (if required) a Data Protection Officer here.]

2. The information we collect

  • Account information — your email address and display name, and authentication data needed to sign you in.
  • Family-link information — your role (student or parent), a family invite code, and the link connecting a student and parent account.
  • Wellbeing data (sensitive / health information) — for student accounts: a daily overall mood rating (1–10), a daily ranking of four life areas (sleep, nutrition, academics, social), optional free-text notes you choose to write, and the dates/times of entries. We treat this as health-related sensitive data and protect it accordingly.
  • Device/technical data — basic information needed to operate the app (e.g., a device push token if you enable notifications in a future version; not collected unless that feature is active).

We practice data minimization: we only collect what's needed to provide the Service. We do not collect location, contacts, advertising identifiers, or biometric data, and we do not buy data about you from third parties.

3. How and why we use your information

  • To provide the core features: let a student record daily check-ins and see their own trends, and let a linked parent view the student's check-ins and trends.
  • To create and manage your account and the family link.
  • To keep the Service secure and working, and to respond to your support requests.

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, automated decision-making, or any unrelated purpose. We will not repurpose data collected for one feature without your further consent.

4. Legal bases (EEA/UK users)

Where the GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we rely on your consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) to operate the Service, and on your explicit consent (Art. 9(2)(a)) to process your wellbeing data, which is special-category health data. You may withdraw consent at any time (see Your Rights); withdrawing consent means we can no longer provide the Service and you can delete your account.

5. The parent can see a student's check-ins

CampusLife is built around a consensual link. When a student joins a family using a parent's invite code, the student's wellbeing entries and trends become visible to that linked parent. This is opt-in (it only happens when the student enters the code) and revocable. A parent in the family can also remove a member. We never enable secret or non-consensual monitoring.

6. Who we share it with

We share your information only with service providers that process it on our behalf, under contracts requiring them to protect it and use it only for us:

  • Supabase — our database, authentication, and hosting provider, which stores your account and wellbeing data on our behalf.

We do not sell or "share" (as defined by California law) your personal information, do not disclose it for cross-context behavioral advertising, do not use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs, and do not provide it to data brokers. We may disclose information if required by law or to protect rights and safety, and in connection with a business transfer (with notice).

7. International data transfers

Your data is stored by Supabase in [HOSTING REGION — confirm]. If you access the Service from outside that region, your data will be transferred and processed there; where required, we use appropriate safeguards (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses).

8. Data retention & deletion

We keep your information for as long as your account is active. We do not retain personal data indefinitely. You can permanently delete your account and all associated data at any time, from within the app: Profile → Delete account. Deletion removes your account, your wellbeing entries, and your family link, and removes your entries from any linked parent's view. If you cannot access the app, email support@campuslife.app from your account email to request deletion (web deletion request: campuslife legal site → Delete your account). Backups containing residual data are purged on our routine backup cycle.

9. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to: access a copy of your data; correct it; delete it; restrict or object to processing; data portability; withdraw consent (including for sensitive/health data) at any time; opt out of sale/sharing/targeted advertising (we do none of these); not be subject to solely-automated decisions (we make none); non-discrimination for exercising your rights; and to appeal a denied request. To exercise any right, use the in-app controls or email privacy@campuslife.app. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority (EEA/UK) or attorney general (US states).

10. Security

We protect your data with encryption in transit (TLS), encrypted on-device storage of session credentials, and row-level access controls that restrict each family's data to that family. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.

11. Children's privacy

CampusLife is intended for users 18 and older. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18 (and specifically anyone under 13, as defined by COPPA). If you believe someone under 18 has created an account, contact privacy@campuslife.app and we will delete it.

12. U.S. state notices (California & others)

We collect the categories described in Section 2, including sensitive personal information (health-related wellbeing data). We use it only to provide the Service (Section 3), retain it as described in Section 8, and do not sell or share it or use sensitive information beyond what's necessary to provide the Service. California residents may exercise the rights in Section 9, including the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information; residents of other US states with privacy laws have comparable rights, including the right to appeal.

13. Consumer health data (Washington, Nevada, and similar)

Your wellbeing data may qualify as "consumer health data." We collect it only with your consent to provide the Service, do not sell it, and do not share it except with the service provider in Section 6 and with the parent you choose to link with.
